South Sikkim Travel Plan - One Day Itinerary

Morning:

Start at Hotel Somras, Chisopani: Begin your day early at Hotel Somras in Chisopani. Enjoy a hearty breakfast to fuel your adventure.

Drive to Namchi (Approx. 1 hour): Head towards Namchi, the capital of South Sikkim, known for its serene beauty and cultural richness.

Samdruptse Monastery: Visit the Samdruptse Monastery, famous for its massive 135-foot tall statue of Guru Padmasambhava. It offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.

Lunch:

Lunch in Namchi: Savor some local Sikkimese cuisine at one of the restaurants in Namchi. Try dishes like momos, thukpa, or traditional Sikkimese thali.

Afternoon:

Char Dham Temple: Explore the Char Dham complex, a significant religious site featuring replicas of four sacred pilgrimage destinations of India - Badrinath, Puri, Rameswaram, and Dwarka.

Temi Tea Garden (Approx. 30 mins drive): Take a picturesque drive to Temi Tea Garden, Sikkim's only tea estate. Stroll through the lush tea plantations and enjoy a cup of fresh tea.

Ravangla (Approx. 1-hour drive): Head to Ravangla, a tranquil town surrounded by mountains and forests.

Evening:

Ravangla Buddha Park: Visit the stunning Buddha Park with its majestic statue of Lord Buddha. Enjoy the peaceful ambiance and panoramic views.

Back to Hotel Somras, Chisopani: Return to Chisopani via Namchi, appreciating the scenic beauty of South Sikkim.

Night:

Dinner at Hotel Somras: Conclude your day with a delicious dinner at Hotel Somras in Chisopani.

West Sikkim Travel Plan - One Day Itinerary

Morning:

  • Start at Hotel Somras, Chisopani: Begin your day with a hearty breakfast at Hotel Somras in Chisopani.
  • Drive to Pelling (Approx. 2-3 hours): Head west to Pelling, one of West Sikkim's most renowned destinations, known for its stunning views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range.

Mid-Morning:

  • Pemayangtse Monastery: Visit the historic Pemayangtse Monastery, a serene Buddhist monastery with intricate woodwork and panoramic views of the Himalayas.

Lunch:

  • Lunch in Pelling: Enjoy a delicious lunch at a local restaurant in Pelling, trying some Sikkimese delicacies.

Afternoon:

  • Rabdentse Ruins: Explore the ruins of Rabdentse, the ancient capital of Sikkim. This archaeological site offers a glimpse into the region's rich history.
  • Khecheopalri Lake (Approx. 1 hour drive): Drive to the sacred Khecheopalri Lake, known as the "Wishing Lake." Take a peaceful walk around the lake and make a wish.

Evening:

  • Return to Chisopani: Head back to Chisopani, enjoying the scenic drive through the picturesque landscapes of West Sikkim.

Night:

  • Dinner at Hotel Somras: Arrive back at Hotel Somras in Chisopani for a relaxing dinner and a comfortable night's stay.

East Sikkim Travel Plan - One Day Itinerary with Stay in Gangtok

Morning:

  • Start at Hotel Somras, Chisopani: Begin your day with a hearty breakfast at Hotel Somras in Chisopani.
  • Drive to Tsomgo Lake (Approx. 2-3 hours): Head east to Tsomgo Lake, a stunning glacial lake surrounded by mountains and located at a high altitude.

Mid-Morning:

  • Tsomgo Lake: Spend your morning at Tsomgo Lake, taking in the breathtaking views and perhaps even enjoying a yak ride.

Lunch:

  • Lunch at a Local Eatery: Enjoy lunch at a local eatery near Tsomgo Lake, where you can savor some traditional Sikkimese cuisine.

Afternoon:

  • Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir: Visit Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir, a revered shrine dedicated to an Indian army soldier who is believed to protect the border even after his death.
  • Nathula Pass (Approx. 30 mins drive): If it's open to tourists (check in advance), visit Nathula Pass, a high-altitude mountain pass on the Indo-China border, offering spectacular views.

Evening:

  • Drive to Gangtok (Approx. 2-3 hours): Head to Gangtok, the capital of Sikkim, where you will be staying for the night.

Night:

  • Check-in at Your Gangtok Hotel: Check-in to your chosen hotel in Gangtok and freshen up.
  • Dinner in Gangtok: Explore the local dining scene in Gangtok and enjoy dinner at a restaurant of your choice.

Overnight:

  • Stay in Gangtok: Rest at your Gangtok hotel for the night, getting ready for more adventures in the days to come.

Discover South & West Sikkim:

South & West Sikkim is a treasure trove of natural beauty, with lush greenery, pristine lakes, and majestic mountains. Some of the highlights of our travel plans include:

  • Gangtok: Explore the capital city with its vibrant markets, monasteries, and breathtaking views of the Himalayas.
  • Namchi: Visit this picturesque town known for its monasteries, gardens, and the awe-inspiring Char Dham.
  • Pelling: Discover the tranquility of Pelling, surrounded by lush forests and stunning views of the Kanchenjunga range.
  • Ravangla: Immerse yourself in the charm of Ravangla, with its tea gardens, monasteries, and panoramic vistas.
  • Yuksom: Experience the historical significance of Yuksom, the first capital of Sikkim, and embark on treks to pristine lakes and monastic sites.
  • Tashiding: Visit the sacred Tashiding Monastery, set against a backdrop of lush green hills, and witness the unique Bumchu festival.

Additional Tourist Places:

South Sikkim:

  • Buddha Park (Ravangla): Also known as Tathagata Tsal, this park features a giant statue of Lord Buddha overlooking the mountains. It's a serene and spiritually significant spot.
  • Tendong Hill: Located near Namchi, Tendong Hill offers stunning panoramic views of the Himalayas and the surrounding countryside. It's a great spot for nature enthusiasts and hikers.
  • Sikip: This offbeat destination on the banks of River Rangeet is perfect for those seeking tranquility. You can enjoy river rafting and bird watching in the area.
  • Ralong Hot Springs: These natural hot springs are located near Ralong and are believed to have therapeutic properties. It's an ideal place to relax and unwind.
  • Kewzing: This small village is known for its traditional Tibetan homestays and offers a glimpse into the local way of life. You can also visit the Kewzing Monastery.

West Sikkim:

  • Sangachoeling Monastery: This historic monastery is perched on a hilltop near Pelling and offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas. It's also a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Dubdi Monastery: Located in Yuksom, Dubdi Monastery is one of the oldest monasteries in Sikkim and has a rich cultural heritage. The trek to reach here is scenic.
  • Khangchendzonga Waterfalls: These waterfalls are a mesmerizing sight, and a short trek takes you closer to their beauty. It's located near Pelling and makes for a refreshing stop.
  • Singshore Bridge: Known as the highest suspension bridge in Asia, the Singshore Bridge offers breathtaking views of lush valleys and forests. It's near Pelling.
  • Kirateshwar Mahadev Temple: Located near Legship, this ancient temple dedicated to Lord Shiva is a sacred site for pilgrims. The annual Bala Chaturdesi festival is celebrated here.
  • Barsey Rhododendron Sanctuary: If you visit during the blooming season (April to May), this sanctuary near Hilley becomes a riot of colors with rhododendron flowers in full bloom.

These additional destinations will further enhance your exploration of South and West Sikkim, offering a mix of natural beauty, spirituality, and cultural experiences. Don't forget to consult local authorities and guides for the latest information and travel recommendations when planning your visit.

East Sikkim (Gangtok)

Nestled in the lap of the mighty Himalayas, East Sikkim is a picturesque and enchanting destination that offers a blend of natural beauty, cultural richness, and adventure opportunities. Located in the northeastern part of India, this region is a must-visit for travelers seeking a unique and serene experience.

South & Weast Sikkim (Namchi)

South & Weast Sikkim is a hidden gem nestled in the eastern Himalayas, offering travelers a tranquil escape into nature's embrace. Known for its lush greenery, cascading waterfalls, and serene monasteries, this region is a paradise for nature enthusiasts and spiritual seekers alike.

West Sikkim (Galsying)

Nestled in the lap of the Himalayas, West Sikkim offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, rich culture, and serene spirituality. This enchanting region, less explored than its eastern counterpart, is a haven for those seeking an off-the-beaten-path experience.
